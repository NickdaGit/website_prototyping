# Introduction to Monkey Magic AI

## What is MonkeyMagic.ai?

MonkeyMagic.ai is a playful, personality-driven website that blends artificial intelligence education with entertainment. It presents itself as the home of a fictional (yet characterful) AI venture — part tech company, part creative playground. The site doesn't take itself too seriously, but beneath the cheeky exterior there is genuine ambition: to make AI concepts accessible, to build fun browser-based games, and to establish a distinctive brand identity that stands apart from the typical corporate tech website.

The domain and name — Monkey Magic — sets the tone immediately. This is a site where monkeys code, dream, and apparently steal bananas. The personality is woven into every section, from the taglines on navigation links to the flavour text on game pages.

---

## The Vibe

The overall vibe is **cheeky curiosity with a wink**. It balances:

- **Playfulness** — Banana references, monkey characters, absurd humour, arcade games with pixel art
- **Warmth** — The team is presented with personality and quirks rather than corporate bios
- **Ambition** — The knowledge section, POC area, and change request system signal that this is a project being actively built, not a static joke site
- **Craftsmanship** — The games are self-contained, polished, and genuinely playable. The jokes are curated into categories with a seeded daily-pick system. There's attention to detail in the touch controls, pixel art, and responsive design.

The aesthetic is clean and modern — white backgrounds with a light corporate feel on the main pages, switching to dark-themed canvas games in the Fun section. The visual identity centres on the Monkey Magic logo and a colour palette of gold/yellow (bananas), blue (tech/trust), and dark tones (game environments).

---

## Sections

### Home (`index.html`)

**Vibe:** Grand entrance with a cinematic feel.

The homepage opens with a full-width hero video overlaid with the site title and tagline: *"Where monkeys code, dream and sometimes steal bananas."* Below the hero sit three section cards — About, Knowledge, Fun — acting as the primary navigation. A search bar at the bottom teases future searchable content.

**Intent:** Give visitors a quick sense of brand personality and route them to the section that interests them. The hero video creates an immediate visual impression. The three cards keep navigation simple and decisive.

### About (`about.html`)

**Vibe:** The team behind the magic — warm, quirky, human.

The About page presents the Monkey Magic AI mission in three pillars (each with a distinct voice), followed by team member cards with photos and personality-driven descriptions. Each team member links to a detailed bio page. The Founder section (Nicholas) gets prominent placement as a single full-width card.

**Intent:** Establish credibility and personality simultaneously. The missions explain what the company does and believes; the team cards put faces and character to the project. The tone avoids corporate sterility — team descriptions mention lederhosen, crystal balls, and banana obsessions.

### Knowledge (`knowledge.html`)

**Vibe:** The ambitious, growing brain of the operation.

Currently showing "Something is brewing" with a link to the Proof of Concepts section. The underlying folder structure is ready for tiered content (beginner, intermediate, advanced) across AI topics. A Proof of Concepts card provides a bridge to experimental work.

**Intent:** This is where Monkey Magic intends to deliver genuine value — educational content about AI, tokenization, prompt engineering, and related topics. The tagline "for curious little monkeys" signals that the tone will be approachable rather than academic. The section is deliberately understated for now, building anticipation rather than launching half-complete.

### Fun (`fun.html`)

**Vibe:** The arcade — loud, colourful, come-and-play.

The Fun page is a grid of cards linking to games and the Joke of the Day. Each card has a title, brief description, and links to a self-contained game page. The current lineup includes Monkey Tetris, Banana Runner, Donkey Kong, Monkey Memory Match, Monkey Pac-Man, and Banana Pong, plus a daily joke picker.

**Intent:** Pure entertainment with a branded twist. Every game is monkey/banana-themed, self-contained in a single HTML file, and designed to work on both desktop and mobile. The games serve multiple purposes: they're fun on their own, they showcase technical capability, and they give visitors a reason to keep coming back. The Joke of the Day system (seeded daily picks from a curated library) ensures fresh content every day without requiring server-side infrastructure.

### Proof of Concepts (`poc/poc.html`)

**Vibe:** The workshop — behind the scenes, hard hats on.

Currently an "Under Construction" page with the message "Hard hats required. We're tinkering behind the scenes." Previously housed homepage variant prototypes and the Donkey Kong game before it graduated to the Fun section.

**Intent:** A staging area for experimental ideas that aren't ready for prime time. The Under Construction framing keeps expectations managed while signalling that active development is happening. Items that prove themselves here get promoted to their proper section.

### Bio Pages (`nicholas.html`, `zara.html`, `gunther.html`, `lila.html`)

**Vibe:** Character spotlights — each team member gets their own stage.

The bio pages expand on the brief card descriptions from the About page. Nicholas's page features a magazine-style banner, a founder video, and detailed personal story. Each bio follows a consistent layout (photo, role, story sections, back link) while allowing individual personality to come through.

**Intent:** Deepen the connection between visitors and the team. These pages reward curiosity — you don't have to visit them, but if you do, you learn more about the people behind the project.
